May se suele utilizar en present tenses, mientras que Might se suele utilizar en past tenses

I thought I may go to the gym

I think I may go to the gym

I thought I might go to the gym

Se utiliza may para algo que es posible o que podría darse (tiene más fundamento, es más factible)

I may have dessert after dinner

Don't tell anybody, but he may lose his job

Se utiliza might para algo más hipotético (tiene menos fundamento, es menos factible)

If you hurry, you might get there on time

If I win the lottery, I might buy a Ferrari

Se utiliza con el pasado simple para expresar un deseo

I wish I was younger

He wishes he were rich

She wishes she were travelling on first class

Nótese que hemos utilizado were en lugar de was con he y she para darle un aire más formal

Se utiliza con would para expresar el deseo de que una situación que nos produce enojo termine

I wish you would turn down the music!

I wish I would turn down the music!

Nótese que el segundo ejemplo está mal, ya que no se puede utilizar el mismo sujeto en ambas partes

Se utiliza con el pasado perfecto para expresar el deseo de haber hecho las cosas de manera distinta en el pasado (arrepentimiento)

I wish I had been paying attention during the lesson, now I feel lost!

Cuando tenemos dos verbos seguidos, el primer verbo es el que determina si el siguiente va en infinitivo o en gerundio

Infinitivos

Utilizamos infintivo más to cuando el primer verbo es want, afford...

I want to pass my exam

We cannot afford to have a swimming pool in our house because they are usually very expensive

Utilizamos infintivo sin to cuando el primer verbo es modal (might, would, rather...) o make y let

Let go on me!

They would help you if you had better manners

Gerundios

Utilizamos gerundio cuando el primer verbo es enjoy, can't help...

I enjoy singing in the shower

She couldn't help laughing

Utilizamos gerundio después de una preposición

Support my channel by becoming a Patreon

I was punished for getting asleep during the lesson

Utilizamos gerundio cuando el primer verbo es un phrasal verb (claro, un prhasal verb se compone de un verbo y de una preposición)

Keep on doing exercise, you look great!

Like, love, hate y prefer

Después de estos verbos lo más común es utilizar gerundio

No obstante, es posible utilizar infinitivo + to y gerundio

Utilizamos gerundio para hablar en general

I like swimming

I prefer cycling to driving

Nótese que drive está en gerundio a pesar ir seguido de to

Utilizamos infintivo + to para hablar de algo en específico

I like to swim first thing in the morning when there aren't many people

You don't need to give me a lift to the station, I prefer to walk

No obstante, utilizamos infinitivo + to si cualquiera de estos verbos fuera seguido de would

I would like to travel abroad

I'd prefer to stay at home tonight

Ambos

Utilizamos infinitive + to o gerund con los siguientes verbos:

It started to rain / It started raining

Remember to lock the door / I remember going to Italy as a child

Sorry, I forgot to do it / I will never forget seeing the Taj Mahal

I tried to open the window / Try calling her on her mobile

Cuando los verbos son begin, start y continue, podemos utilizar infinitive + to o gerund sin que su significado varíe

It started to rain / It started raining

He continues to work in the company / He contiues working in the company

You need to clean the car /

No obstante, en algunos casos el significado puede variar

Remember + infinitive » Primero recuerdas, luego haces algo (Remember to lock the door. Recuerda que debes cerrar la puerta y luego la cierras)

Remember + gerund » Primero haces algo, luego lo recuerdas (I remember going to Italy as a child. Primero fuiste a Italia y luego lo recordaste)

Forget + infinitive » No recordaste hacer algo (I forgot to do it)

Forget + gerund » Hiciste algo que no puedes olvidar (I will never forget seeing the Taj Mahal)

Try + infinitive » Hacer el esfuerzo para conseguir algo (I tried to open the window)

Try + gerund » Prueba a ver si algo funciona (Try calling her on her mobile)

Need + infinitive » Algo que necesitas o que necesitas hacer (You need to clean the car)

Need + gerund » Es, por narices, una pasiva (The car needs cleaning (passive voice) = The car needs to be cleaned)

The car needs to clean

he learned to ride horses

he learned horse riding (en gerundio, el verbo actúa como sustantivo)

Se utiliza usually + verb in infinitive para algo que solemos hacer en el presente

I usually go to sleep very soon

I usually drink coffee after reading the news

Se utiliza used to + verb in infinitive para algo que solíamos hacer en el pasado, pero ahora no

I used to get up at six o'clock every morning

I always used to do the houseworks every weekend

Se utiliza be used to + verb in gerund para algo que se ha convertido en una rutina en la actualidad

I'm used to working in a team

He's finally used to living in our country

Se utiliza get used to + verb in gerund para algo que todavía no se ha convertido en una rutina, estamos en el proceso

It's very hard to get used to working out every afternoon

I can't get used to sleeping in complete darkness during the night, I'm terrified!

Se utiliza ought to / should have + verb in past participle cuando creemos que alguien no hizo lo correcto o para expresar o arrepentimiento

It's my fault. I ought to have told you earlier that my mother was coming.

We've gone the wrong way. We shouldn't have turned left at the traffic lights.

Se utiliza may / might have + verb in past participle cuando creemos que algo es verdad o que ha sucedido (probabilidad)

He still haven't arrived. You may not have given him the right directions.

Somebody might have stolen your wallet when you were getting off the train.

Se utiliza must have + verb in past participle cuando estamos seguros o casi seguros de que algo es verdad o que ha sucedido (certeza)

I must have left my phone at Anna's. I definitely remember having it there.

You must have seen something. You were there when the accident happened.

Se utiliza can't have + verb in past participle cuando estamos seguros o casi seguros de que algo es imposible o que no ha sucedido

She can't have gone to bed. It's only ten o'clock!

You can't have seen their faces very clearly. It was too dark.

Se utiliza couldn't have + verb in past participle cuando las especulaciones tratan de un tema del pasado

She couldn't have gone to bed. It was only ten o'clock!

You couldn't have seen their faces very clearly. It was too dark.

Se utiliza can't have + verb in past participle como la forma negativa de must have + verb in participle

I mustn't have left my phone at Anna's. I definitely remember having it somewhere else.

I can't have left my phone at Anna's. I definitely remember having it somewhere else.

You mustn't have seen anything. You weren't there when the accident happened.

You can't have seen anything. You weren't there when the accident happened.

No obstante, el resto de casos admite forma negativa (ought not to / oughtn't to, should not / shouln't, may not o might not)
